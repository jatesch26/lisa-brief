What's driving it
A cold front stalled over the Florida peninsula and won't move until Thursday. Strong high pressure over the Mid-Atlantic tightens the offshore gradient, driving persistent NE onshore flow against westerly flow aloft. NWS QPF (the authoritative number, not the raw model output) puts total Mon–Thu accumulation at about 2.5" for the MCO area — well within NWS's stated 1–3" interior forecast range. Tuesday has the highest daily amount (~0.74") but it's distributed through the whole day. Wednesday is lighter (~0.45"). Thursday adds another 1.0"+ but most of that falls afternoon and evening after departure. Coverage loads up overnight each night, which means the parks get partial clearing cycles between active periods.
💨 Tuesday: NE 15–25 mph, gusts to 35 mph. Best day for indoor queues and character dining.
