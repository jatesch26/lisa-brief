What's driving it: A cold front stalls near central Florida Monday–Tuesday while a subtropical jet sets up over the Gulf. The NWS notes broad synoptic-scale lift is possible as Florida sits in the left-forward quadrant of the jet — a setup that can produce enhanced rainfall and prolonged storm coverage. Guidance confidence is low, but multiple models agree on the basic pattern.

Wednesday winds: The stronger continental high sitting north of Florida tightens the pressure gradient behind the front, driving progressively stronger NE onshore flow. The NWS statistical guidance already gives much of Central Florida a 30–40% chance of 40 mph gusts Wednesday. Coastal and beach impacts are likely.

Thursday outlook: Rain chances begin to ease (40%) as drier air works southward, with highs recovering to 79°F and NE winds 10–15 mph. The worst of it should be Tuesday–Wednesday.

Practical notes: Outdoor plans Tuesday–Wednesday should have indoor backup options. Atlantic beach areas will have rough surf and potentially dangerous conditions — high rip current risk was already flagged Friday and continues through the weekend. Wednesday could see conditions described as "hazardous to dangerous" for boating. Check NWS Melbourne updates Sunday night for refined timing and totals as the front approaches.
