What's driving it
A cold front stalls over the Florida peninsula Monday and lingers through Thursday. Strong high pressure over the Mid-Atlantic tightens the offshore pressure gradient, driving persistent NE onshore flow against westerly flow aloft — a classic convergent band setup. NWS notes NBM 90th-percentile accumulations near 6" along the coast for the Tue–Thu window, with 3–4" and locally 5"+ considered reasonable. The Disney area (interior) is expected to see 1–3" — lower than the coast but still meaningful. Coverage and totals peak during overnight hours each night.
